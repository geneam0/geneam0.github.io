Tinkercad Table Cleaner

  • Gene Lam
  • Friday, June 12, 2020 7:56pm

During my fourth semester, I participated in the Zahn Apprenticeship program, where I worked with interdisciplinary engineers on client-driven projects, and gained skills in CAD, manufacturing, and electronics. As an Electrical Engineering Apprentice, I worked with my pod on a table cleaning robot, brainstorming ways for it to move around and dispense liquid. The program also hosted workshops where I learned skills in KiCad, CNC, and a certification in Arduino. It was also nice learn from upperclassmen at the Grove School of Engineering. Quang, a senior Electrical Engineer, helped me understand the Zener diode when I was struggling in Electronics.

Unfortunately, due to the coronavirus, the Apprentice program was shifted online and so were the projects. The electronics architecture of the table cleaner was moved to TinkerCad, an online simulator. In a way, it was more convenient as we didn't have to buy the components. However, there would've been more to gain from working and troubleshooting the parts in-person. Regardless, we didn't have a choice.

I started the Table Cleaning robot with the distance sensors. From there, I added motors and motor drivers to control the movements of the robot through code. In the code, I utilized 2D array to monitor the location of the robot as it "moved" through its space. The distance sensors served to track initial position and the motors told distance traveled, allowing us to understand where the robot needed to "clean." I also added a function for the robot to deal with obstacles. When the robot finishes cleaning the table, it plays the Jigglypuff tune from Pokemon.
